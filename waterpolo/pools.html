🏆 Premier Championship Facility - Coronado

Brian Bent Memorial Aquatics Complex is one of Southern California's premier aquatic facilities, featuring championship-caliber pools with electronic timing systems. Named after former Coronado High School water polo player Brian Bent, this $8.5 million facility opened in 2006 and has become a nucleus for high-performance swimming and water polo teams.

The complex features two pools: a competition pool (8 lanes, 50 meters or 20 lanes 25 yards) and an instructional pool (6 lanes, 25 yards). Located on Coronado High School campus, it attracts 300+ daily visitors for training and therapy, rising to 500-600 during events.

🏟️ Championship Tournament Venue - El Cajon

Granite Hills High School Aquatic Center features an Olympic-sized pool facility dedicated in March 2012. This 40,000 square foot complex was the first Olympic-size pool on an East County high school campus. The facility serves as a hub for East County community groups, youth aquatics teams, and CIF water polo championships.

The venue features a unique 50-meter by 25-yard pool configuration, different from other district pools. Located in El Cajon with easy freeway access, it provides excellent tournament hosting capabilities with modern amenities and ample spectator seating.

🌟 KJVR Memorial Tournament Host - Norco

Norco High School serves as the primary venue for the prestigious Kennedie June Von Ryan Memorial Tournament. This facility provides an excellent competition environment with quality aquatic facilities and strong community support for water polo events.

The venue holds special significance as the home of the KJVR Memorial Tournament, honoring the memory of Kennedie June Von Ryan and supporting student athletes through scholarship opportunities. Home to the Norco Cougars, the facility combines competitive excellence with meaningful community impact.

🏆 US Club Championship Venue - Riverside

Riverside Polytechnic High School features premier aquatic facilities that serve as a primary venue for the US Club Championship tournament. As the oldest high school in Riverside (tracing heritage to 1887), this historic institution combines tradition with modern championship-level facilities.

The current facility on Victoria Avenue opened in September 1965 and has been enhanced with competition-grade aquatic amenities. The venue provides central access for teams throughout Southern California, with multiple pool configurations allowing efficient tournament scheduling.
