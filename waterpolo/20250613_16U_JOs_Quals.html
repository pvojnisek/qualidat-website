🏊‍♂️ Junior Olympics Qualification Tournament

This tournament serves as the qualification event for the Junior Olympics, bringing together 16 teams from across California to compete for coveted spots in the national championship. Teams are divided into four groups (A, B, C, D) for round-robin play, followed by a re-bracket system (AA, BB, CC, DD) that ensures competitive matches at every level.

The unique format allows every team to play meaningful games throughout the weekend, with brackets designed to create fair competition between teams of similar skill levels. Tournament features 51 total games across three days utilizing dual venue pools.

🏆 Shores Black Matches

Undefeated Group C winners advancing to Championship bracket as the #3 seed. Dominated group play with commanding victories and impressive goal differential, establishing themselves as serious contenders for the Junior Olympics qualification.

Current Group Standings

Rankings based on completed matches. Teams are ranked by match points (3 for win, 1 for tie), then goal differential, then goals scored. Group winners advance to Championship bracket (AA), runners-up to Classic bracket (BB).

Re-bracket Format

After group stage completion, teams are re-seeded based on their group placement into four new brackets. This ensures competitive balance and meaningful games for all participants throughout the tournament.

🥇
Junior Olympics Qualification
This tournament serves as the official qualification pathway for the USA Water Polo Junior Olympics National Championship. Top finishers earn coveted spots at the premier national competition where the best 16U teams compete for the ultimate prize.
🎯
Qualification Stakes
Championship bracket (AA) teams automatically qualify for Junior Olympics. Classic bracket (BB) teams compete for additional at-large berths. Strong performance here determines national tournament seeding and provides crucial preparation against elite competition.
🏊‍♂️
BBMAC - Coronado
Brian Bent Memorial Aquatics Complex features two championship-caliber pools with electronic timing systems. This premier facility hosts opening day matches and serves as the primary venue for Friday's group stage competition in a world-class aquatic environment.
🏟️
Granite Hills High School
Located in El Cajon, this venue hosts the majority of tournament action across Saturday and Sunday. With two competitive pools and excellent spectator facilities, it provides the perfect setting for intense bracket competition and championship finals.
🌟
National Championship Impact
Junior Olympics represents the pinnacle of youth water polo, featuring the nation's top 16U teams. Qualification through this tournament provides teams exposure to college recruiters, elite competition experience, and potential advancement to international youth programs.
🚀
College Recruitment Opportunity
Top performers at Junior Olympics gain significant visibility among NCAA Division I recruiters. Many players who excel at this level receive scholarship offers from prestigious water polo programs including Stanford, USC, UCLA, and Cal Berkeley.
